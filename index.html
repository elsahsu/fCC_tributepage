Mr. Chiune Sugihara


The Sugiharas with Yukiko's sister and Chiune's aide are shown in this picture.

Timeline of Sugihara's Life:


  • 1900 -Born on January 1 in Gifu Prefecture, Japan. He enters Waseda University in 1918 and majors in English language.

  • 1919 -Passes the Foreign Ministry Scholarship exam and starts official diplomatic training.

  • 1923 -Assigned to Harbin, China where he later becomes an expert on Russian affairs. There he marries Klaudia Semionovna Apollonova and is converted to Russian Orthodox Church.

  • 1935 -Marries Yukiko and later they have four sons

  • 1939 -He becomes a vice-consul of the Japanese Consulate in Kaunas, Lithuania. Duties include reporting on Soviet and German troop movements and finding out if Germany plans an attack on the Soviets. Meanwhile, Germans invade Poland and World War Two starts.
  • 1940 -Decides to issue visas for thousands of Jews despite that Japanese Foreign Ministry insists grant no visa for the ones who lack of proper travel documents and sufficient fund. He handwrites visas from July 18 to September 4, when he has to leave because the consulate is closed. According to witnesses, he still writes visas while in transit from his hotel and after boarding the train at the Kaunas Railway Station, throwing visas into the crowd of desperate refugees out of the train's window even as the train pulls out.

  • 1944 -Soviet troops enters Bucharest, where he is based at that time. The Sugiharas are captured and imprisoned in a POW camp for eighteen months. Later they are released in 1946 and returns to Japan.

  • 1945 -World War Two ends

  • 1947 -Resigns from foreign service. His wife Yukiko says he is dismissed because of the incident in Lithuania.

  • 1960-1975 -He takes a series of menial jobs to support his family. Later he works for a Japanese trading company in Moscow while his family stays in Japan.

  • 1984 -Israel recognizes him as Righteous Among the Nations

  • 1986 -Passes away
